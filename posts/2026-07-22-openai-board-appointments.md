## What changed

OpenAI appointed David Vélez and Robin Vince to the boards of both the OpenAI Foundation and OpenAI Group PBC (OpenAI's two governing entities).

David Vélez founded Nubank, a Latin American digital bank that now serves more than 135 million customers. He previously worked at Sequoia Capital, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and General Atlantic, and runs a philanthropic organization, VélezReyes+, focused on democratic governance and education.

Robin Vince is Chairman and CEO of BNY, a financial services firm founded over 240 years ago. He spent 26 years at Goldman Sachs before joining BNY in 2022, and sits on several nonprofit boards.

Bret Taylor, Chair of OpenAI's boards, said their experience "will be invaluable as OpenAI serves more businesses and people around the world."

## Why it matters

This is a governance update rather than a product change, but it's a signal of direction: both appointees come from large-scale financial services rather than technology, which fits OpenAI's continued push into enterprise and business products (like ChatGPT Work and ChatGPT for Small Business, also announced this week). It doesn't change anything for users of OpenAI's products directly.

## How to use it

No action needed — this is a leadership announcement, not a feature or policy change.
