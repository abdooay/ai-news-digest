## What changed

On July 20, 2026, Anthropic announced a new grants program focused on rare genetic diseases, part of its wider "AI for Science" initiative. The goal is to build a community of researchers using Claude to speed up work on diseases that affect small patient populations and often get less research funding than common conditions.

The program has two tracks:

- **Basic science** — for teams that combine clinical researchers, patient organizations, and data scientists to study how a disease works. Anthropic is partnering with the Monarch Initiative, which provides tools like the Mondo Disease Ontology (a standardized way of naming and classifying diseases) and the DisMech library.
- **Biotech** — for early-stage biotech companies, supporting tasks like writing documentation, analyzing treatment strategies, and preparing regulatory filings.

Each recipient can get up to $50,000 in Claude API credits over six months, usable with Claude Opus or other approved "biology models." Anthropic says some projects may qualify for exemptions from its biosecurity classifiers (automated filters that restrict certain biology-related requests) if their work would otherwise be blocked.

## Why it matters

Rare diseases, by definition, affect few patients, which historically means less commercial incentive to research them. Free API access lowers the cost barrier for academic labs and small biotech teams that want to use Claude for tasks like literature review, data analysis, or drafting regulatory documents, without needing a grant budget of their own.

## How to use it

- Eligible applicants: academic researchers doing basic rare-disease research, or early-stage biotech companies developing rare-disease treatments.
- Application deadline: **August 2, 2026, 11:59 PM PST**.
- Apply through Anthropic's [application form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfwDGfVg2lHJ0cc0oF_ilEnjvr_r4_paYi7VLlr5cLNXASdvA/viewform).
