## What changed

On July 22, 2026, Anthropic launched a connector (a plug-in that gives Claude access to an external data source) for the Anthropic Economic Index. The Economic Index is Anthropic's public dataset that tracks patterns in how people actually use Claude across different jobs and tasks — it has previously been used by researchers, journalists, and policymakers studying AI's effect on work.

With the connector turned on, you can ask Claude questions like "Which occupations use AI the most?" or "What are the most common ways people in Colorado use Claude?" and get answers grounded in the real Index data, instead of a general answer from the model's training.

Anthropic is clear about a limitation: the Index reflects patterns in Claude usage specifically, not the labor market as a whole. Claude is expected to mention this limitation when relevant during a conversation.

The connector works across all Claude models, in any conversation, and Anthropic says it takes about a minute to set up. The full underlying datasets remain freely downloadable from Anthropic's website as before; the connector just makes them queryable in plain language.

## Why it matters

Before this, using the Economic Index meant downloading a dataset and analyzing it yourself, or reading Anthropic's own published reports about it. Now anyone can just ask Claude a question in plain English and get an answer sourced from the actual data. This is useful for journalists, researchers, or curious users who want a quick, sourced answer about how AI is being used in a particular industry or occupation, without doing their own data analysis.

## How to use it

- Go to claude.ai and open the connector menu (where you add data sources to a conversation).
- Add the Anthropic Economic Index connector — Anthropic says this takes about a minute.
- Ask a question about AI usage patterns, e.g. "Which occupations use AI the most for coding tasks?"
- Remember that answers reflect Claude usage patterns, not the full labor market.
