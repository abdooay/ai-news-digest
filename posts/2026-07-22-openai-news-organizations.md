## What changed

On July 22, 2026, OpenAI published a roundup describing how news organizations are using its technology across reporting, audience growth, and business operations. It's a collection of examples in publishers' own words, rather than a new product release.

The piece confirms two funding commitments: continued support for the American Journalism Project's portfolio of publications (spanning 38 U.S. states), and continued support for the Lenfest Institute for Journalism and WAN-IFRA (the World Association of News Publishers). It also describes publishers using OpenAI's tools to make large archives of past reporting searchable, translate coverage into other languages and formats, and speed up editorial and business workflows.

## Why it matters

This isn't a new feature or model — it's OpenAI documenting an existing relationship with the news industry, at a time when AI's effect on journalism (traffic, licensing, trust) is a live and contentious issue. The concrete facts here are the renewed funding commitments; the rest is case-study framing supplied by OpenAI and the organizations it names.

## How to use it

There's no new product to try here. If you work at a news organization and want to look into these partnerships:
- The American Journalism Project's supported portfolio spans 38 U.S. states.
- The Lenfest Institute for Journalism and WAN-IFRA are named as continuing partners.
