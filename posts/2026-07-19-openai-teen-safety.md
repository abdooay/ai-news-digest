## What changed

On July 16, 2026, OpenAI published an article making its case for why teenagers should have access to AI tools like ChatGPT, alongside a summary of the safety measures it has built for teen users. OpenAI says nearly 9 in 10 teens who use ChatGPT in a given week do so for learning, finding information, building a skill, or getting work done.

The article groups OpenAI's teen safety approach into two parts: automated guardrails that adjust what the AI will do based on a user's likely age, and parental controls that let a parent manage a teen's account directly. On age detection, OpenAI uses an age-prediction model that looks at account and usage signals — such as how long an account has existed and its typical activity patterns — to guess whether an account likely belongs to someone under 18. If it isn't confident, it defaults to the more restricted under-18 experience, and gives adults a way to verify their age if they're wrongly flagged.

## Why it matters

Governments and parents have been pushing AI companies to protect younger users. This article is OpenAI's public explanation of its approach: give teens access, rather than blocking it, but pair that access with protections built specifically for their age. For parents, it means there are concrete settings available to limit and monitor how a teen in their family uses ChatGPT. For anyone building on OpenAI's models, it signals that age-based restrictions and content rules are an active, ongoing part of the platform rather than a one-time policy.

## How to use it

- Age prediction runs automatically on consumer ChatGPT accounts; there's nothing to turn on. If an adult account gets flagged as under-18 by mistake, OpenAI provides a way to verify your age and unlock the full adult experience.
- Parents can link their account to their teen's account and then set parental controls, including quiet hours (times when ChatGPT can't be used), turning off voice mode, turning off memory, removing image generation, and opting the teen's conversations out of model training.
