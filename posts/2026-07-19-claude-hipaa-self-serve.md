## What changed

As of July 14, 2026, Anthropic made HIPAA configuration self-serve for Claude organizations. HIPAA is a US law that sets rules for protecting patient health information. Companies that want to use Claude with that kind of data need a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) — a legal contract stating the vendor, in this case Anthropic, will handle the data according to HIPAA's rules.

Previously, setting this up required contacting Anthropic directly. Now, an eligible admin in either Claude Enterprise (Claude for organizations) or the Claude Platform (the API) can review the BAA, download an implementation guide, and turn on the HIPAA configuration themselves, in one flow.

## Why it matters

This mainly matters for healthcare organizations, or any company handling protected health information, that wants to use Claude. Removing the manual back-and-forth with Anthropic means these organizations can get HIPAA-ready faster. It applies to both Claude Enterprise accounts and to companies building on the API, so it covers chat-based use and developer use.

## How to use it

- An eligible admin should go to their organization's settings, in either Claude Enterprise or the Claude Platform console.
- Look for the HIPAA readiness option, review and accept the Business Associate Agreement (BAA), download the implementation guide, and enable the configuration.
- Check the Claude Help Center articles "HIPAA-ready Enterprise plans" and "HIPAA readiness for Claude API" for the specific steps and eligibility requirements for your account type.
