## What changed

On July 23, 2026, OpenAI began rolling out "Health in ChatGPT" to logged-in ChatGPT users aged 18 and older in the U.S., on web and iOS, across the Free, Go, Plus, and Pro plans. It is not yet available in Codex (OpenAI's coding agent).

Users can choose to securely connect Apple Health and supported medical records. Once connected, ChatGPT can use that information — like medications, lab results, recent visits, sleep, and activity — inside normal conversations, not just in a separate "Health" section. For example, ChatGPT could take a food allergy into account while planning a meal, or a recent injury into account while suggesting weekend activities.

This is a change from how the feature worked before: OpenAI had already tested a dedicated health space with a limited group of users, but found that more than 70% of health-related conversations happened outside that dedicated space anyway. So they moved the capability into regular chat, with a "Health" entry in the sidebar remaining as a home base for connecting data, reviewing what's stored, and browsing past health conversations.

OpenAI says connected medical records, Apple Health data, and conversations that use them are not used to train its models or to target ads.

## Why it matters

This removes a step: previously you had to go into a specific health mode to get ChatGPT's help with your medical history. Now that context can follow you into any chat. It also means ChatGPT's answers about your health can be grounded in your actual records instead of generic advice, provided you choose to connect that data.

The obvious tradeoff is privacy: this only works if you connect real medical and health-tracking data to your ChatGPT account. OpenAI states this data isn't used for training or ads, but users should still weigh comfort with keeping this data connected against the convenience it provides.

## How to use it

- Open **Health** from the left sidebar in ChatGPT.
- Connect Apple Health and/or supported medical records.
- Review and edit what's stored, so it stays accurate.
- After that, mention health-related context in any normal conversation (not just inside the Health section) and ChatGPT can use the connected data if relevant.
- Available now to U.S. users 18+ on web and iOS, on Free, Go, Plus, and Pro plans. Not available in Codex.
