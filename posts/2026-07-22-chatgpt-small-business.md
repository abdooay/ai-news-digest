## What changed

OpenAI launched the "ChatGPT for Small Business" program, aimed at business owners who handle many roles themselves — marketing, accounting, sales, and operations. It has four parts:

- **Training**: product-focused webinars covering ChatGPT Work (OpenAI's agent-style tool for multi-step work tasks) for accounting, marketing, and ecommerce, plus in-person "AI academies" held around the US. OpenAI says 78% of participants at last year's Small Business AI Jams events built a working AI workflow in a single day.
- **Educational resources**: customer stories, interactive guides built for ChatGPT Work, and short videos meant to help owners get started quickly.
- **Partner integrations**: collaborations with Dropbox, Shopify, Intuit, Slack, and Wix, offering curated skills and promotions built for small-business workflows.
- **ChatGPT Work itself**: described as able to handle multi-step tasks such as turning a voice note into a formatted message, analyzing customer reviews, or spotting new product opportunities from market data. It's powered by GPT-5.6 and available on every ChatGPT subscription plan and device.

## Why it matters

Small business owners often don't have time to figure out how to apply a general-purpose AI tool to their specific work. This program packages that setup work — training, guides, and ready-made integrations with tools many small businesses already use — so the barrier to trying ChatGPT Work is lower. It's also a sign OpenAI is investing directly in the small-business segment rather than only targeting large enterprises.

## How to use it

- If you run a small business and use (or are considering) ChatGPT, look for the "ChatGPT for Small Business" program page for webinar schedules and in-person AI academy locations near you.
- If you already use Dropbox, Shopify, Intuit, Slack, or Wix, check whether the partner integrations offer a relevant skill or promotion for your plan.
- ChatGPT Work is available on all subscription plans, so no separate purchase is needed to try it.
