## What changed

Anthropic introduced **Claude for Teachers**, a version of Claude aimed at verified K-12 (kindergarten through 12th grade) teachers in the United States. It is free for individual teachers who sign up by June 30, 2027, which gives them a full year of access.

The tool can:
- Plan a lesson using high-quality instructional materials, matched to a state's teaching standards.
- Adapt ("differentiate") the same material for students at different skill levels.
- Look at class data — rosters, diagnostic test results, attendance — and use it to inform teaching decisions.
- Run repeated tasks automatically, such as reviewing students' "exit tickets" (short end-of-class check-ins) every day.

It also connects to nine education platforms teachers already use, including Canva Education, MagicSchool, and Brisk Teaching, and pulls standards-aligned content from a service called Learning Commons, which covers standards for all 50 US states.

## Why it matters

Teachers often spend unpaid personal time on lesson planning and grading. A tool that plans lessons, adapts them for different students, and automates repetitive review tasks could cut into that workload — if it works as described. Because it's free and tied to real state standards rather than generic content, it's aimed specifically at classroom use rather than general-purpose chat.

Data privacy is also addressed directly: Anthropic says Claude for Teachers data is not used to train its models, and the product is covered by a K-12 Data Processing Addendum written to comply with FERPA, the US federal student-privacy law. The terms were also developed with input from the American Federation of Teachers, a US teachers' union.

## How to use it

- Eligibility: verified K-12 educators in the US only.
- Cost: free for individuals.
- Sign-up deadline for a full year of free access: June 30, 2027.
- Sign up through Anthropic's Claude for Teachers page (linked below) with school/teacher verification.
