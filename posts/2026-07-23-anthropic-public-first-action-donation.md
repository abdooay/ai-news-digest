## What changed

On July 21, 2026, Anthropic announced it is donating another $20 million to Public First Action, a political group that Anthropic describes as "nonpartisan." This brings Anthropic's total support for the group to $40 million. Public First Action's work includes educating the public about AI and engaging lawmakers on both sides of the political aisle on AI safety measures.

Anthropic tied the timing to rapidly advancing AI capabilities. It pointed to its own Claude Mythos Preview model, which the company says found thousands of high-severity software vulnerabilities in major operating systems and browsers earlier in 2026. That model was released through Project Glasswing, a program giving cybersecurity experts early access to models like this specifically so they can fix vulnerabilities before they're exploited by attackers.

The donation supports Anthropic's "Advanced AI Framework," a policy proposal that calls for:

- Government verification of AI companies' own safety claims.
- Civil penalties for unsafe practices.
- Government authority to restrict deployment of models that pose catastrophic risk.
- Mandatory testing and public transparency requirements for frontier (leading-edge) AI developers.
- Export controls on advanced semiconductors.
- Measures against illicit access to AI models.

Anthropic says both this donation and the earlier $20 million only fund public education and policy work — not campaigns for or against specific candidates.

## Why it matters

This is a straightforward political-spending story: Anthropic is putting more money behind advocacy for its preferred AI regulations, at a moment when AI companies are increasingly spending on policy fights over how strictly the US should regulate the technology. For anyone tracking AI policy, it signals Anthropic sees mandatory safety testing and government oversight authority as priorities worth funding lobbying and public-education efforts around, not just talking about in blog posts.

## How to use it

This is a policy and funding announcement, not a product change — there's nothing to install or configure. If you follow AI policy, the practical takeaway is to watch for Public First Action's public education campaigns and lobbying activity referencing Anthropic's Advanced AI Framework.
