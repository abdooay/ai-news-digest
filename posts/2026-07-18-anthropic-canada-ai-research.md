## What changed

Anthropic is committing **$10 million CAD** (Canadian dollars) to fund "beneficial and responsible" AI research at eight Canadian institutions:

- Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute (Amii), Edmonton
- Mila, Montréal
- Vector Institute, Toronto
- CHEO and the CHEO Research Institute (a children's hospital)
- Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH)
- Université Laval's Institute for Intelligence and Data
- University of Saskatchewan
- University of Toronto Data Sciences Institute

Instead of cash grants, most partners receive **Claude API credits** — pre-paid usage of Anthropic's API — to support their research teams. The University of Toronto's Data Sciences Institute, for example, will hand out those credits through a scientific-review process, meaning researchers apply and are judged on their proposals.

Separately, Anthropic is adding Amii, Mila, and Vector to its **Anthropic for Startups** program this summer. That program gives at least $5,000 USD in API credits to each of the hundreds of startups affiliated with those three institutes.

## Why it matters

This is a resource commitment (API access, not just money) aimed at Canadian researchers and startups working on things like reinforcement learning, AI safety, health applications, sustainability, and low-resource languages including Quebec French and Indigenous languages. For researchers or startups affiliated with these institutions, it's a concrete way to get free or subsidized access to Claude models for actual research and product work, rather than a general grant announcement with no clear path to access.

## How to use it

- If you're affiliated with one of the eight listed institutions, contact your institution's research office about applying for Claude API credits (the University of Toronto's Data Sciences Institute uses a scientific-review application process; other institutions may differ).
- If your startup is affiliated with Amii, Mila, or Vector, watch for onboarding into the Anthropic for Startups program this summer, which provides at least $5,000 USD in API credits.
